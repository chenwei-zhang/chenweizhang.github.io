--- title: "Revisiting Plasmonic Properties of Complex Semiconductor Nanocrystals Using Magnetic Circular Dichroism Spectroscopy: A Cautionary Tale" excerpt: "
This is one of my MSc's projects that I led as a coauthor.
In this work we comparatively investigate the plasmonic properties of these semiconductor nanocrystal materials using magnetic circular dichroism (MCD) spectroscopy, owing to the highly specific response of localized surface plasmon resonance (LSPR) to magnetic field and light polarization. In all systems, we find indisputable evidence that the LSPR absorption bands have been mischaracterized. The MCD signals that coincide with the absorption bands previously assigned to LSPR of GFO and CuxFeyS2-zSez NCs show rapid saturation with magnetic field and independence on the sign of charge carriers, indicating that the corresponding absorption bands are not due to LSPR. This behavior is consistent with intra-ionic and inter-band transitions for GFO and CuxFeyS2-zSez NCs, respectively. The MCD signal corresponding to the absorption spectrum of WO3-x NCs shows Brillouin function dependence on magnetic field at high photon energies (attributed to d-d transitions of W5+) and linear dependence in the near-infrared range (attributed to LSPR), indicating that broad absorption spectrum of WO3-x NCs is only partly associated with LSPR. The results of this work underline the challenges in assigning absorption bands of complex semiconductor nanocrystals to LSPR, and demonstrate that MCD spectroscopy is an invaluable tool for characterization of these materials.

This work was submitted to Chem. Mater. in 2022." collection: research --- This paper was submitted to Chemistry of Materials. in September, 2022.
